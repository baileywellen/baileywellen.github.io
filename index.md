# Improving health services through efficient large data processing.

<p class="lede">I am a data scientist studying mental and behavioral health services with a focus on treatment access, system- and policy-level implementation barriers, and responsible use of health data.</p>

I earned undergraduate degrees in Computer Science and Data Science from [Carthage College](https://www.carthage.edu/) and am now pursuing a Ph.D. in Computational and Data Sciences at [Washington University in St. Louis](https://wustl.edu/). My work uses electronic health records and administrative data to identify disparities in substance use disorder treatment access. I care deeply about algorithmic fairness, reducing systemic barriers to treatment, and improving healthcare professional well-being while delivering evidence-based patient care.  

I am honored that my dissertation work has been funded by an F31 training grant (F31DA066305) and T32 institutional grant from the National Institute on Drug Abuse. I am open to postdoctoral opportunities related to health services, health policy, and mental/behavioral health starting in Fall 2027 or Spring 2028.
