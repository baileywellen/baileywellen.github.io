# Research

<p class="section-intro">Using data to understand—and improve—access to compassionate, effective substance use care.</p>

My research sits at the intersection of addiction health services, public policy, and computational data science. I study how health systems can better support people with substance use disorders, particularly by identifying where treatment access breaks down and which changes can make care more equitable and feasible in practice.

## Research interests

- **Addiction health services:** access to and quality of care for people with opioid and other substance use disorders.
- **Health equity:** the structural, insurance-related, and geographic factors that shape treatment access and patient outcomes.
- **Electronic health records and administrative data:** using large, real-world datasets to understand care pathways, health-care teams, and barriers to treatment.
- **Responsible data science:** algorithmic fairness, meaningful stakeholder engagement, and translating findings into implementable improvements.

I am especially interested in research that connects rigorous quantitative methods with the lived experiences of people navigating health systems.
